This chapter explores the vitamin and mineral requirements of individuals between the ages of 19 and 50 years. The impact of excessive intake of vitamins and minerals is of interest and importance to the general public. Toxicology is concerned with the detection of toxicants in food, water, air, and soil while nutrition is focused on the essentiality of vitamins and minerals for human health. This review includes the tolerable upper limits of nutrient intake, which have been developed by various health organizations with a focus on risk potential. This historical review also provides a perspective on the maximum level of daily nutrient intake that is likely to cause a potential risk following the consumption of food and/or supplements containing these essential nutrients.
