  The fractures encountered were more complex than usual, occurring very distal. This made interpretation of plain film radiographs difficult. A high level of clinical suspicionas well as the use of CT scanning proved useful for the diagnosis as well as pre-operative planning. Without this, it might have been easy to misdiagnose and mistreat the injury. Open reduction and Kirshner wiring was used in all three cases due to the level of comminution.
