Maurice Anthony Biot was born in Antwerp, Belgium, on May 25, 1905. He attended the University of Louvain and received bachelor's degrees in Thomistic Philosophy (1927), Mining Engineering (1929), and Electrical Engineering (1930). In 1931 he was awarded a D.Sc. With support from the Belgian American Educational Foundation, Biot spent 2 years at Caltech (1931–1933), where he graduated with a Ph.D. in Aeronautical Science in 1932. Subsequently, he went on to become a faculty member at Harvard (1934–1935), and then at Louvain (1935–1937) and Columbia (1937–1946). While he was Professor of Mechanical Engineering at Columbia, Biot was appointed to a post in the National Defense Program, and he received leave of absence to join the Aeronautical Department of the California Institute of Technology in 1940, where he worked on government research programs with Theodore von Karman. From 1946 to 1952 Biot was on the faculty at the Brown University. Subsequently, he acted as a consultant to various government agencies and industrial laboratories: mainly at the Cornell Aeronautical Laboratory, Shell Development, and Mobil Research and Development.
